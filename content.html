My Story

I first played with code when I was 7 years old after dear old Dad bought me a Commodore64 from an old man down the street. A box with hundreds of games on flimsy black floppy disks came with it so, needless to say, I spent more hours with it than anything else.

I thoroughly enjoyed coding as a boy, teen and adult, returning to it more frequently than anything else throughout my life but (frustratingly) didn't pursue it as a profession.

While spending most of last year inside, I realized a possible opportunity to shift my career away from IT and into the world of software development.

I spent thousands of hours researching common software development technologies and the profession itself. I studied from books, articles, and online communities, completing many instructor-led courses offered online. I explored anything interesting, completing dozens of course projects and some of my own for multiple platforms including embedded, desktop, mobile, games, and web development. I also studied clean code, problem solving techniques, test driven development, design patterns, refactoring, and software construction.
